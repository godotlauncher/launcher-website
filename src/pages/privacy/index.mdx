# Privacy Policy

_Last updated: July 10, 2026_

Godot Launcher ("we", "us", or "our") respects your privacy. This Privacy
Policy explains how information is processed when you use
[https://godotlauncher.org](https://godotlauncher.org) or its documentation at
[https://docs.godotlauncher.org](https://docs.godotlauncher.org).

## 1. Who We Are

Godot Launcher is an open source project maintained by Mario Debono and
contributors. For privacy questions or requests, contact
[privacy@godotlauncher.org](mailto:privacy@godotlauncher.org).

## 2. Information We Process

The website does not provide user accounts, contact forms, subscriptions, or
mailing-list signup forms. We do not ask you to submit account or profile data.

Our hosting and analytics providers may process limited technical and usage
information, including:

- Pages and links visited
- Download and command-copy interactions
- Time spent on pages, clicks, scrolls, and mouse movements
- Browser, device, operating system, and screen information
- Approximate location inferred from an IP address
- Referrer, page URL, performance, and diagnostic information
- A pseudonymous browser or session identifier when analytics consent is given
- Basic hosting logs and request metadata

Microsoft Clarity may also process page structure and interaction information
to provide heatmaps and session playback. Text entered into input fields is
masked by Clarity. This website does not use Clarity's Identify API to send a
custom user identifier.

The documentation site uses Algolia DocSearch. Search terms and related
technical request information are sent to Algolia when you use documentation
search. We do not enable Algolia Insights behavioral event tracking.

## 3. Cookies and Consent

We use a consent tool provided by 68 Publishers to store your cookie choices
and communicate them to Google Analytics and Microsoft Clarity through Google
Consent Mode. Non-essential analytics cookies are disabled unless you consent
to analytics storage.

You can accept or reject analytics cookies from the consent banner. You can
review or withdraw your choice at any time using the `Cookie Settings` link in
the site footer. Withdrawing consent prevents further consent-based analytics
storage and causes the website to clear supported first-party analytics
cookies.

The website and documentation use separate consent configurations, so your
choice may need to be made separately on each site. For the cookie inventory,
purposes, lifetimes, providers, and browser controls, see our
[Cookie Policy](/cookies).

## 4. Analytics Services

### Google Analytics 4

We use Google Analytics 4 to understand aggregate website usage and measure
interactions such as installer downloads and Winget command copies. Google
Analytics receives the consent state selected in the cookie banner. When
analytics storage is denied, Google Consent Mode prevents Analytics from
reading or writing analytics cookies. Google may still receive limited,
cookieless measurement signals where supported by Consent Mode.

Google's processing is governed by its
[Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy) and
[Google Analytics data safeguards](https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/6004245).

### Microsoft Clarity

We use Microsoft Clarity to understand how visitors navigate and interact with
the website through aggregate insights, heatmaps, and session playback.
Clarity reads the Google Consent Mode state produced by our consent tool. When
analytics consent is denied, Clarity operates without analytics cookies and
limits features that require a persistent visitor or session identifier.

Clarity playback data is generally retained for 30 days. Click and heatmap
data, and sessions that are labeled or favorited, may be retained for up to 13
months. For more information, see
[Microsoft Clarity data retention](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/setup-and-installation/data-retention)
and the [Microsoft Privacy Statement](https://privacy.microsoft.com/privacystatement).

### Algolia DocSearch

We use Algolia DocSearch to provide search on the documentation site. When you
perform a search, Algolia processes the search terms and technical request
information needed to return results. Algolia Insights is disabled, so the
documentation site does not send separate search-result click or conversion
events to Algolia. See [Algolia's Privacy Policy](https://www.algolia.com/policies/privacy/).

## 5. Purposes and Legal Basis

We use analytics to understand website performance, improve content, identify
usability problems, and measure whether download instructions are effective.
Where consent is required, the legal basis for non-essential analytics storage
and related processing is your consent. You may withdraw consent at any time
without affecting processing that occurred before withdrawal.

Essential processing, including serving the websites, documentation search,
basic security, hosting logs, and remembering your consent choice, may be
necessary to provide the requested service or may be based on our legitimate
interests in operating and securing it, as permitted by applicable law.

## 6. Hosting and International Processing

The website and documentation site are hosted on GitHub Pages, a static-site
hosting service provided by GitHub, Inc. GitHub may process request logs and
technical information as part of providing and securing the hosting service. See
[GitHub's Privacy Statement](https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/privacy-policies/github-privacy-statement).

GitHub, Google, Microsoft, and Algolia may process information in countries
outside your country of residence. Their privacy documentation describes the
transfer mechanisms and safeguards they use for international processing.

## 7. Your Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights concerning personal data,
including rights to:

- Request access, correction, deletion, or restriction
- Object to certain processing
- Receive portable data where applicable
- Withdraw consent at any time
- Lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority

Because the website does not provide accounts or directly collect submitted
profile information, we may not be able to associate analytics records with a
named individual. Provider-specific rights and controls may also be available
through Google, Microsoft, GitHub, or your browser.

For privacy requests, contact
[privacy@godotlauncher.org](mailto:privacy@godotlauncher.org).

## 8. California Privacy Rights

California residents may have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act
(CCPA), as amended, including rights to know, correct, or delete personal
information and to opt out of its sale or sharing where applicable.

We do not sell personal information. We do not knowingly use website analytics
to collect sensitive personal information or build named user profiles. For
related requests, contact
[privacy@godotlauncher.org](mailto:privacy@godotlauncher.org).

## 9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when our website, providers, or legal
obligations change. The updated version will be posted here with a revised
"Last updated" date.
